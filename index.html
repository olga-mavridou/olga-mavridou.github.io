Lingo Powers — Microlearning Video Series

CEFR A1–B1

Instructional Designer / Content Creator · 06/2025 – 09/2025

Context

An online foreign-language tutoring center commissioned a set of French microlearning videos for beginner adult learners. I worked within a small team — a fellow instructor on content and curriculum, an Instructional Lead on visual and creative review, and the center's owners on final approval.

Challenge

Turn an agreed curriculum into 18 individually scripted, structured video lessons — each with clear objectives, bilingual support and a matching printable workbook — on a tight production schedule.

Design Process

  • Co-selected the coursebook and divided units with a fellow instructor, then defined learning objectives and a clear communicative goal for each video
  • Wrote instructional scripts and slide sequences — greeting, objective preview, guided explanation, practice — for every module
  • Produced bilingual (Greek/French) narration and recorded both language versions myself
  • Built in-video exercises and a separate six-page printable workbook per unit, using AI-assisted tools for text and exercise generation within the Instructional Lead's existing templates
  • Submitted each video for creative review, incorporated feedback, then sent to the center's owners for final sign-off before moving to the next module
  • Mid-project, adapted all 18 videos to a second version without the client's branding, at the owners' request, so the material could be resold independently in future

Solution

18 microlearning video modules (with a branded and unbranded version of each) plus 18 matching six-page printable workbooks, covering beginner French vocabulary and grammar.

Outcome

A complete, approved course library delivered on schedule despite a mid-project scope change — my first collaborative, paid instructional design project, and one I'm genuinely proud of.

Tools — Canva · InVideo AI · ChatGPT · Claude

Due to confidentiality agreements, selected screenshots and a short video excerpt are available on request.

Project Constraints

This project was developed within an established instructional framework. Visual design templates, branding guidelines and production standards — including the workbook format — were predefined by the Instructional Lead. My role focused on instructional design, scripting, narration and content development within that existing design system.

i-Εκπαίδευση / QLearn — French Learning Ecosystem

CEFR A1–B2

Instructional Designer / Tutor · 06/2022 – Present

Context

An ongoing engagement with a private tutoring center — which also offers secondary-school tutoring, foreign languages, computer certifications and university-partnership programmes — across three of its platforms over time: live online classes (4Schools), a self-paced course library (Moodle-based i-Εκπαίδευση), and — from 2025 — a commercial course marketplace (QLearn). My work focused specifically on its French-language courses.

Challenge

Build one coherent, reusable set of French learning materials that could serve live teaching, self-paced study and — eventually — a paid public course, without duplicating the work each time.

Design Process

  • Selected the Vite et Bien coursebook and built original grammar and vocabulary PDF guides for every chapter
  • Ran live online lessons via 4Schools, curating existing online exercises to use in-class and assign as homework
  • Structured the same core content into a self-paced course library on the center's Moodle platform, organised by CEFR level (A1–B2)
  • Wrote learning objectives and self-study guidance so the material could stand on its own, without a live instructor
  • In 2025, adapted the existing library into a paid, standalone course and published it on QLearn, the center's new open course marketplace

Solution

Original grammar & vocabulary guides · curated live-class exercises · a structured self-paced Moodle course · a published, purchasable course on QLearn.

Outcome

A single body of material that grew with the platform — from live classroom support to a self-paced resource to a commercial product — supporting adult learners' independent study and engagement at every stage.

Platforms — 4Schools · Moodle (i-Εκπαίδευση) · QLearn (from 2025)

Self-paced French A1 course — i-Εκπαίδευση (Moodle)

What I'd improve

I'd like to add simple completion tracking to the self-paced course, so I can see which resources learners actually use and refine the ones that don't land.

04

Design Philosophy

how I approach a course

ADDIE

Analyze, Design, Develop, Implement, Evaluate — my structural backbone. I understand the learner and the objective before I build anything.

SAM

Successive Approximation Model. I prototype early and refine through rounds of feedback, rather than waiting for one "final" draft.

Adult Learning

Adults learn by doing, not by being told. I design for autonomy — content learners can control, revisit and apply immediately.

Learning Experience Design

Instructional design meets UX. A course should feel as easy to navigate as it is to learn from.
